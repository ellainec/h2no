The year is 2018. It is summer, and a massive heatwave has struck the city of Vancouver. It hasn't rained in over a month, and half of the reservoirs' water supply has already been depleted. All across the city, precious H20 is being wasted daily. Water sprinklers being left on constantly, faucets left on and forgotten, and cars being washed more frequently than necessary. These are just a few examples of the many ways that water is being mistreated every day. To combat this blatant disregard for H20-kind, and in hopes of saving the city from certain disaster, an automated aquatic advocate named "H-2NO" has been created and sent to the parts of the city where the water wasting activity is most dense. H-2NO is a master of turning off taps and faucets; however, it has one fatal flaw: It is highly susceptible to water. Even the tiniest drop of water making contact with its outer shell will cause it to short circuit and shut down. Guide H-2NO through water-rampant residential neighbourhoods and across sprinkler-infested lawns to the main sources of water waste, while putting an end to all unnecessary water usage along the way.
